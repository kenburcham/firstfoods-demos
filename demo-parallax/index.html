Columbia Basin Fish Accords

In 2008, CTUIR signed the Columbia Basin Fish Accords along with Warm Springs, Yakama, Colville tribes and BPA, securing $900 million for salmon restoration projects throughout the Columbia River Basin. The First Foods Policy Department counts this as one of their greatest achievements.

Habitat Restoration Projects

In 2009, the Fisheries department kicked off restoration projects across the 5 subbasins. Habitat restoration is an essential part of the First Foods plan to protect and enhance fish populations by providing healthy habitat and reducing stream tempturatures for fish viability. More fish mean more harvest opportunities.

Forest Management Plan

In 2010, the RAF department completed the Forest Management Plan for CTUIR to re-vitalize forest management after long delay in addressing resouce management challenges.

Water Pollution Laws Enacted

CTUIR First Foods Policy department collaborated with Oregon Department of Environmental Quality and the EPA to set new standards for fish consumption to better protect Indian health. In 2011, water quality standards were adopted by Oregon and approved by the EPA that increased the daily fish consumption rate from 17.5g to 175g based on Indian fish consumption rates. This is very significant because fish consumption is used to calculate allowable polution in the water.

Data Management Improvement Project

In 2013-14, DNR partnered with GIS and others to conduct a comprehensive data management needs assessment. Groups across DNR worked to standardize their protocols and parameters and built a system for storing and reporting data called the CDMS. Improved data quality and accessibility is resulting in better informed decisions for natural resource management.

Record Return of Lamprey

Lamprey restoration work kicked off in 2012 and in 2015 saw a dramatic increase in returns. More than 400 adult lamprey returned to the Umatilla river, breaking the previous year's record of around 300.

Agricultural Management Plan

In 2016, the Range, Ag. and Forestry department completed their Agricultural Management Plan that provides direction and management targets for soil and crop resources on the Umatilla Indian Reservation.
